Fredrik and September presenting PINK DRESS

100 * 120 CM

Art for the masses, bought by the brave

I paint abstract, expressionistic work in loud colors.
Not because I’m cheerful. Because subtlety can be dishonest.

I don’t start with concepts, sketches, or a comforting plan. I start when something refuses to stay quiet. The canvas is not a safe space—it’s a negotiation that usually escalates. Control exists, but only after things get messy.

Color is doing the heavy lifting. It carries tension, irritation, clarity, and the occasional bad mood. If a painting feels balanced, it’s probably lying. I’m not interested in harmony; I’m interested in pressure—what happens just before things look like they might fall apart.

Music is always present. It is the music that inspire me. Always.

I paint in periods, not habits. Long silences followed by intense bursts. Inspiration is unreliable, but when it shows up, it expects full attention and leaves a mess.

This isn’t art designed to calm a room.
It’s art that occupies it.
If it makes you slightly uncomfortable, that’s not a side effect—it’s quality control.
